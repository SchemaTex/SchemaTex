## Scenario

A case manager at a substance abuse recovery center creates this ecomap during an initial biopsychosocial assessment. The diagram externalises the client's full ecological field — every system pressing on his recovery — and immediately surfaces the imbalance: most energy flows inward (legal supervision, sponsor), almost nothing flows outward. The visual becomes the conversation starter for the treatment plan.

## Annotation key

- `===` — strong, supportive connection
- `==` — moderately strong connection
- `---` — tenuous or fragile connection
- `~~~` — stressful, conflicted connection
- `-/-` — severing or cut-off relationship
- `- - ` — currently restricted (supervised visits shown with spaces)
- `-->` — one-way energy or support flow (giver → receiver)
- `<->` — reciprocal, mutual relationship
- `category: mental-health / family / legal / work / substance` — colors the node by domain
- `importance: major` — increases node prominence in the layout

## How to read

The client sits at the center. Arrow directions show who gives energy to whom: the probation officer and sponsor both point *toward* James (external pressure / support), while James gives energy *toward* the AA group. The therapist is the only `<->` reciprocal relationship — the treatment alliance. The `-/-` (severing) line to the dealer marks the highest-risk edge in early recovery. Supervised visits (`- -`) with the children shows the family system is strained but not severed.
